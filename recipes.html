Carbonara

Carbonara is a classic Italian pasta dish originating from Rome. It typically features spaghetti or other long pasta tossed with a creamy sauce made from eggs, Pecorino Romano cheese, pancetta (or guanciale), and black pepper. The dish is known for its rich, silky texture and intense flavor, achieved without the use of cream. The heat from the pasta is used to cook the eggs gently, creating a smooth, cohesive sauce that clings to the noodles.
